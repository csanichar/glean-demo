# Volta Coffee Co. — New Barista Training Guide

**Document ID:** TRAIN-001  
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-20  
**Owner:** Retail Operations  
**Classification:** Internal Use Only

## Training Overview

All new baristas complete a structured 2-week training program before working independently on bar. Training is conducted at the barista's assigned location under a Certified Trainer (shift lead or manager who has completed the Train-the-Trainer program).

### Week 1: Foundations

| Day | Focus | Duration |
|-----|-------|----------|
| 1 | Orientation, food safety (ServSafe), POS basics | 8 hours |
| 2 | Coffee fundamentals: seed to cup, Volta sourcing philosophy | 4 hours |
| 2 | Tasting and palate development (cupping session with trainer) | 2 hours |
| 3 | Espresso: grinder calibration, dialing in, extraction theory | 8 hours |
| 4 | Espresso practice: pulling 100+ shots to build muscle memory | 8 hours |
| 5 | Milk steaming: technique, temperature control, latte art basics | 8 hours |

### Week 2: Menu & Service

| Day | Focus | Duration |
|-----|-------|----------|
| 6 | Full menu execution: core beverages (MENU-001) | 8 hours |
| 7 | Signature beverages, pour-over, cold brew service | 6 hours |
| 7 | Batch brew procedures, water quality checks | 2 hours |
| 8 | POS proficiency, order sequencing, bar workflow | 8 hours |
| 9 | Shadowed bar shift with trainer (full service) | 8 hours |
| 10 | Certification assessment (see below) | 4 hours |

## Certification Assessment

At the end of Week 2, the trainee must pass a practical assessment to be cleared for independent bar work.

### Assessment Components

1. **Espresso Calibration:** Dial in the grinder and pull 3 consecutive shots within spec (BREW-001). Assessed on: dose accuracy, yield accuracy, time consistency.

2. **Beverage Execution (5 drinks):** The trainer orders 5 random beverages from the menu. Assessed on: recipe accuracy, milk texture, temperature, presentation, latte art (minimum tulip for lattes and flat whites).

3. **Speed Test:** Prepare 4 different beverages simultaneously within 6 minutes. Assessed on: workflow efficiency, drink quality under pressure, cleanliness.

4. **Knowledge Quiz:** 20 questions covering:
   - Extraction parameters for espresso, batch brew, and cold brew
   - Water quality targets
   - Milk steaming temperatures and limits
   - Menu recipes and sizing
   - Food safety basics
   - PTO and scheduling procedures
   
   Passing score: 85% (17/20)

5. **Customer Service Role Play:** Trainer presents 3 scenarios:
   - A customer complaint about drink quality
   - An order modification request (dietary restriction)
   - A rush scenario with a line of 5+ customers

### Certification Outcome

- **Pass:** Barista is cleared for independent shifts. Certification date is logged in the employee portal.
- **Conditional Pass:** Barista passed most components but needs additional practice in 1–2 areas. Extended training (2–3 additional days) with re-assessment.
- **Fail:** Rare. Indicates fundamental skill gaps. Training is extended by one full week. If the second assessment also fails, the Store Manager and People Ops determine next steps, which may include reassignment to a non-bar role.

## Ongoing Development

### Monthly Skills Check

Every barista receives a brief (15-minute) monthly skills check from their Shift Lead covering:
- Espresso quality (pull 2 shots, check spec)
- Milk steaming (1 latte, assessed on texture and temperature)
- One randomly selected menu beverage

Results are logged in the employee portal. Two consecutive below-standard checks trigger a retraining session.

### Latte Art Progression

Volta expects baristas to develop their latte art skills over time:

| Timeframe | Expected Level |
|-----------|---------------|
| Certification (Week 2) | Tulip |
| 3 months | Rosetta |
| 6 months | Swan or tulip variants |
| 12 months | Free pour proficiency for competitions |

Monthly latte art throwdowns (friendly competitions) are held at each location on the last Friday of the month. Participation is optional but encouraged. Winners receive a $25 gift card.

### Advanced Certifications

After 6 months, baristas may pursue:
- **SCA Barista Skills Foundation:** Volta covers the exam fee ($100). Study materials are in the Training Resources folder on Google Drive.
- **SCA Brewing Foundation:** Same coverage. Recommended for baristas interested in pour-over and batch brew quality.
- **Certified Trainer:** After 12 months and manager recommendation. Requires completing the Train-the-Trainer program (2-day workshop held quarterly at the roastery).

## Dress Code

- Volta-branded apron (provided) must be worn during all shifts.
- Clean, solid-color shirt underneath (black, white, gray, or navy). No logos or graphics.
- Closed-toe, non-slip shoes required (Volta provides a $75 annual shoe allowance via expense reimbursement).
- Hair must be secured if shoulder-length or longer. Beard nets required for beards longer than 1 inch.
- Minimal jewelry: stud earrings only, no rings (hygiene), watches permitted.
- No strong fragrances — this interferes with coffee tasting and can affect the customer experience.
