## Sourcing Philosophy

Volta Coffee Co. sources exclusively specialty-grade coffee (SCA score 80+) through direct trade relationships and vetted importers. We prioritize long-term partnerships with producers who demonstrate quality consistency, environmental stewardship, and fair labor practices.

### Sourcing Criteria

All coffee purchased by Volta must meet the following minimum standards:

1. **Quality:** SCA cupping score of 80 or above, verified by our QA team (protocol in QA-001).
2. **Traceability:** Origin traceable to the farm or cooperative level. Lot-level traceability preferred.
3. **Fair Pricing:** Volta pays a minimum of $0.50/lb above the C-market price for all purchases. For coffees scoring 85+, the premium is $1.00–$2.50/lb above C-market, negotiated annually based on quality.
4. **Environmental Standards:** Producers must demonstrate at least one of: organic certification, Rainforest Alliance certification, or documented shade-grown/agroforestry practices.
5. **Labor Practices:** No child labor. Fair wages verified through importer audits or direct visits. Volta conducts origin visits to each active partner at least once every 2 years.

## Sustainability Commitments

### Carbon Footprint

Volta is committed to reducing our carbon footprint by 30% by 2028 (baseline: 2023).

Key initiatives:
- **Shipping:** Prioritize sea freight over air freight for all green coffee. Air freight is used only for micro-lots under 500 lbs when time-sensitive.
- **Roasting:** The Loring S35 Kestrel smart roaster at Red Hook recirculates heat and reduces emissions by approximately 80% compared to traditional drum roasters.
- **Packaging:** Retail bags are 100% recyclable (kraft paper with a plant-based valve). Moving to compostable bags by Q4 2026 (vendor evaluation in progress).
- **Operations:** All locations use LED lighting and Energy Star equipment. Williamsburg and Park Slope locations have switched to 100% renewable energy through ConEd's Clean Energy program.

### Waste Reduction

- **Coffee Grounds:** Partnered with NYC Compost Project. All locations have composting bins for spent grounds. Monthly pickup.
- **Cup Program:** $0.50 discount for customers bringing reusable cups. Goal: 20% of drinks served in reusable cups by end of 2026 (current: 12%).
- **Milk Waste:** Steaming in measured pitchers to reduce oversteaming waste. Training updated in TRAIN-001.
- **Food Waste:** End-of-day pastries donated to City Harvest. Tracking in the Waste Tracker (Notion).

### Community Investment

- **Producer Support Fund:** 1% of gross green coffee purchases are allocated to a fund for producer-level investments (processing equipment, drying beds, water treatment). Disbursed annually based on proposals submitted by our sourcing partners.
- **Barista Education Fund:** $10,000 annual budget for employee coffee education (SCA certifications, origin trips, competition sponsorship).
- **Local Partnerships:** Each location maintains a relationship with one local nonprofit. Current partners: Brooklyn Roasting Project Youth Program (Williamsburg), Park Slope Civic Council (Park Slope), Henry Street Settlement (LES), Red Hook Initiative (Red Hook).

## Green Coffee Inventory Policy

### Par Levels

Maintain a rolling 6-week supply of each active SKU based on the 8-week moving average consumption. Adjust par levels monthly during seasonal demand shifts (cold brew consumption triples in summer, reducing hot beverage bean usage by approximately 15%).

### Contract Structure

- **Annual contracts** with Finca El Porvenir (Colombia) and Fazenda Progresso (Brazil) — our blend base components. Pricing locked annually.
- **Spot purchases** for single-origin offerings (Ethiopia, Kenya, Costa Rica, Guatemala). Purchased per lot based on cupping evaluation.
- **Forward contracts** placed 3–6 months ahead for known seasonal offerings (e.g., Kenya in Q1, Ethiopia naturals in Q2).

### Inventory Tracking

Green coffee inventory is tracked in the inventory-service (see ENG-001 for system details). Key fields:
- Lot number
- Origin and producer
- Arrival date
- Moisture content at intake
- Cupping score
- Quantity remaining (lbs)
- Storage location (warehouse zone)

The data-pipeline generates a weekly Inventory Health Report sent to the Sourcing Manager and Head Roaster every Monday at 8:00 AM.
